High viscosity prepolymers feature a thicker consistency, making them ideal for applications requiring robust adhesion and mechanical properties, such as coatings and sealants. Conversely, low viscosity prepolymers offer better fluidity, facilitating easy application in processes like casting and molding. Both types cater to various industries, including automotive, construction, and consumer goods, emphasizing their versatile performance characteristics.</p></p>
