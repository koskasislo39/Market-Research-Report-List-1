<p><p>Cockpit Voice Recorders (CVRs) are essential devices used in aviation to capture audio from the cockpit, enhancing safety and investigation processes. The market can be divided into two main types: Embedded Type and Ordinary Type. Embedded Type CVRs are integrated within the aircraft's system, offering seamless operation and enhanced durability. In contrast, Ordinary Type CVRs are standalone devices, typically used in older aircraft or for retrofitting, providing basic recording functionalities without extensive integration. Both types play crucial roles in accident analysis and pilot training.</p></p>
